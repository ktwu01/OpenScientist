## When
The correct single-path answer is uncertain and the downside of being wrong is high (research program bets, investment allocation, immigration strategy). Pressure to "pick one" exists but is premature.

## Decision
**Run multiple tracks concurrently rather than choosing.** The domain applications differ but the structure is the same: (a) file O-1A and L-1A in parallel rather than sequential (no statutory prohibition, different standards, adjudicators don't cross-reference); (b) invest in QC exposure via diversified large-caps (Google/IBM/MSFT) rather than pure-plays (IonQ/Rigetti) when timeline is 10–30 years uncertain; (c) pursue EB-1A, EB-1C, and EB-5 tracks simultaneously since no limit on concurrent petitions. The cost of maintaining a second track is usually much less than the cost of one track failing without a backup.

Rejected — "optimize for the most likely path": conflates probability with expected value; high-variance paths with a strong backup dominate lower-variance single-path under uncertainty.

## Local Verifiers
- Are the tracks genuinely independent (different failure modes) or correlated (all fail together)? Parallel tracks only hedge if failure modes are uncorrelated.
- Does parallel execution require contradictory commitments (e.g., stating different facts in different petitions)? If yes, the tracks are mutually exclusive and can't be parallelized honestly.
- What's the marginal cost of the second track relative to the marginal value? Often the second track costs 10–30% extra and doubles your probability of success.

## Failure Handling
If resources are truly too limited for multiple tracks, at minimum document the "next best" path so that if the primary track fails, you can pivot without starting from scratch. The switching cost dominates the time lost.

## Anti-exemplars
- Filing only O-1A because "it's the strongest case" — loses L-1A's same-day Canadian border adjudication as a fallback.
- All-in on a single architectural research bet (pure Mamba or pure Transformer) — hybrid approaches like Nemotron-H exist precisely because the pure bets hedge each other badly.
- Research lab betting its entire agenda on a single paradigm-shift hypothesis — same failure mode.
