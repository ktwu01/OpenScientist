## Fact
In a rigorous review of environmental modelling studies, statements that ML-driven models "achieve higher metric scores" are not acceptable as general conclusions unless they are bounded by benchmark conditions and interpreted through the mechanisms or inductive biases relevant to the task.

## Evidence
During rubric-based review in this session, a broad sentence claiming better ML metric performance was explicitly flagged as a rule violation alongside leaderboard framing. The compliant rewrite narrowed claims to specific inquiry categories and avoided unconditional superiority language.

## LLM Default Belief
LLMs often default to "state-of-the-art" rhetoric: if reported metrics are higher, they summarize the result as general superiority. That shortcut is methodologically wrong for heterogeneous scientific literatures.

## Expiry Signal
Expire or weaken this memory if the review is based on a single standardized benchmark suite with matched protocols, or if the field adopts consensus comparison standards that make cross-study superiority claims methodologically defensible.
